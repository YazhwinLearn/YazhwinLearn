    Overview

    The Post-Harvest Handling and Storage Course offers comprehensive training in the techniques and technologies for handling, processing, and storing agricultural produce after harvest. Participants will learn about post-harvest loss prevention, quality maintenance, and value addition methods to enhance food security, marketability, and profitability in the agri-food supply chain.

    How it Works

    1
    Step 1

    Introduction to Post-Harvest Handling

    Begin by understanding the principles and practices of post-harvest handling, including harvesting, cleaning, sorting, grading, and packaging of agricultural produce to maintain quality, freshness, and market value.

    2
    Step 2

    Storage Technologies

    Explore different post-harvest storage technologies and facilities, such as cold storage, controlled atmosphere storage, drying systems, and packaging methods, to minimize post-harvest losses and extend the shelf life of agricultural products.

    3
    Step 3

    Quality Control and Management

    Learn about quality control and management practices in post-harvest handling, including inspection, testing, monitoring, and certification procedures, to ensure compliance with food safety standards and market requirements.

    Reasons to Choose Post-Harvest Handling and Storage Course

    Minimize Post-Harvest Losses

    Learn techniques to minimize post-harvest losses and improve the quality and marketability of agricultural products, reducing food waste and increasing profitability for farmers and food processors.

    Extend Shelf Life

    Explore storage technologies and preservation methods to extend the shelf life of perishable crops, fruits, and vegetables, ensuring a consistent supply of fresh and high-quality produce to consumers throughout the year.

    Ensure Food Safety

    Understand food safety standards and regulations related to post-harvest handling and storage, implementing best practices and quality control measures to prevent contamination, spoilage, and microbial growth in agricultural products.

    Optimize Market Value

    Enhance the market value and competitiveness of agricultural products by maintaining their quality, appearance, and nutritional value through proper post-harvest handling, storage, and packaging techniques tailored to market demands.

    Improve Supply Chain Efficiency

    Improve efficiency and reliability in the agricultural supply chain by implementing post-harvest handling and storage practices that minimize product losses, reduce transportation costs, and ensure timely delivery to markets and consumers.
