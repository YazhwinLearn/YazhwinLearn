    Overview Steel Structures Design

    Steel structures design focuses on the planning and construction of buildings, bridges, and other structures using steel as the primary structural material. This course covers the fundamental principles of structural steel design, including load calculations, material properties, connection design, and fabrication techniques. Students will learn how to optimize steel structures for strength, durability, and cost-effectiveness while meeting design requirements and safety standards.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Properties of Structural Steel: Understanding the mechanical properties of structural steel, including strength, stiffness, ductility, and durability.
    • Types of Steel Structures: Overview of different types of steel structures, including buildings, bridges, towers, and industrial facilities, and their design considerations.
    • Structural Elements: Study of structural elements commonly used in steel construction, including beams, columns, trusses, and connections.
    • Design Loads and Load Combinations: Analysis of design loads, such as dead loads, live loads, wind loads, and seismic loads, and their combination for structural design.
    • Limit States Design: Introduction to limit states design philosophy and principles, including strength limit states, serviceability limit states, and stability considerations.
    • Steel Material Specifications: Familiarization with steel material specifications and standards, including ASTM, AISC, Eurocode, and other international codes and guidelines.
    • Structural Analysis Methods: Overview of structural analysis methods for steel structures, including static analysis, dynamic analysis, and finite element analysis.
    • Design Codes and Standards: Understanding the provisions of design codes and standards for steel structures, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements and industry best practices.
    • Introduction to Software Tools: Introduction to software tools for structural analysis and design of steel structures, such as SAP2000, ETABS, STAAD.Pro, and Tekla Structures.
    • Case Studies: Analysis and evaluation of real-world steel structure projects, including buildings, bridges, and industrial facilities, to understand design challenges and solutions.
    • Structural Design Philosophy: Understanding the principles of structural design, including safety, economy, and functionality, and their application in steel construction.
    • Design Criteria and Loading Conditions: Analysis of design criteria and loading conditions for steel structures, considering different occupancy categories, environmental factors, and serviceability requirements.
    • Structural Member Design: Sizing and proportioning of structural members, including beams, columns, braces, and connections, based on strength and stability requirements.
    • Connection Design: Design of steel connections, including bolted connections, welded connections, and moment-resisting connections, ensuring adequate strength, stiffness, and ductility.
    • Plate Girder Design: Design principles and methods for plate girder design, including web and flange design, shear and bending resistance, and buckling considerations.
    • Composite Construction: Introduction to composite construction using steel and concrete, including composite beams, composite columns, and composite slabs.
    • Buckling Analysis: Analysis of buckling behavior in steel structures, including Euler buckling, flexural buckling, lateral-torsional buckling, and global buckling, and methods for stability analysis.
    • Design for Seismic Resistance: Considerations for seismic design of steel structures, including seismic force-resisting systems, ductility requirements, and detailing for seismic performance.
    • Design for Wind Loads: Analysis and design for wind loads on steel structures, including wind effects, wind load calculations, and wind-resistant design measures.
    • Design Optimization: Techniques for optimizing the design of steel structures, balancing performance, cost, and constructability considerations to achieve efficient and economical designs.
    • Sustainable Design Practices: Integration of sustainable design principles in steel construction, including material selection, energy efficiency, and life cycle assessment.
    • Resilient Design Strategies: Strategies for designing steel structures to withstand extreme events and hazards, such as earthquakes, hurricanes, floods, and fire.
    • Modular Construction Techniques: Introduction to modular construction techniques using prefabricated steel components, including off-site fabrication, assembly, and transportation.
    • Digital Fabrication and BIM Integration: Integration of digital fabrication technologies and Building Information Modeling (BIM) in steel construction, streamlining design, fabrication, and construction processes.
    • Innovative Steel Structural Systems: Exploration of innovative steel structural systems and technologies, such as long-span structures, tensegrity structures, and deployable structures.
    • Performance-Based Design: Application of performance-based design principles in steel structural engineering, considering functional requirements, performance objectives, and risk assessment.
    • Steel Sustainability Standards: Overview of sustainability standards and certifications for steel construction, including LEED, BREEAM, and Green Globes, and their implications for design and construction practices.
    • Case Studies and Project Presentations: Analysis and presentation of case studies and projects showcasing innovative steel structural design solutions and best practices.
    • Emerging Technologies: Exploration of emerging technologies and materials shaping the future of steel construction, such as high-strength steels, nanostructured materials, and 3D printing.
    • Professional Development and Career Opportunities: Career pathways and professional development opportunities in steel structural engineering, including licensure, certifications, and continuing education.
