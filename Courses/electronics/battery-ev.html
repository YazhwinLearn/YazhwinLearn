    Overview Battery Technology for Electric Vehicle Course

    Battery technology for electric vehicles (EVs) explores the principles, materials, and technologies used in designing batteries for electric vehicle applications. This course covers topics such as lithium-ion chemistry, battery pack design, thermal management, and charging infrastructure. Students will learn how to evaluate battery performance, safety, and reliability for electric vehicle integration and deployment.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of Electric Vehicles (EVs): Introduction to electric vehicle propulsion systems and the role of batteries in powering electric vehicles.
    • Energy Storage Fundamentals: Understanding the basic principles of energy storage, including electrochemistry, energy density, power density, and efficiency.
    • Battery Chemistry: Exploration of battery chemistry and materials, including lithium-ion batteries, nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries, and emerging battery technologies.
    • Battery Components: Understanding the components of a battery cell, including electrodes, electrolytes, separators, current collectors, and casing materials.
    • Battery Management Systems (BMS): Overview of battery management systems for electric vehicles, including state-of-charge (SOC) estimation, state-of-health (SOH) monitoring, and thermal management.
    • Charging Infrastructure: Understanding electric vehicle charging infrastructure, including charging standards, connector types, charging modes, and fast charging technologies.
    • Range and Performance: Factors influencing electric vehicle range and performance, including battery capacity, energy efficiency, vehicle weight, aerodynamics, and driving conditions.
    • Environmental Impact: Considerations for the environmental impact of battery production, use, and disposal, including resource depletion, carbon emissions, and recycling.
    • Market Trends and Innovations: Exploration of market trends and innovations in electric vehicle battery technology, including cost reduction, energy density improvement, and solid-state batteries.
    • Future Perspectives: Discussion of future perspectives and challenges in electric vehicle battery technology, including research directions, policy implications, and market dynamics.
    • Battery Modeling Approaches: Overview of battery modeling approaches, including empirical, equivalent circuit, electrochemical, and physics-based models.
    • Modeling Parameters: Identification and estimation of battery model parameters, including electrical parameters, thermal parameters, and aging effects.
    • State-of-Charge (SOC) Estimation: Techniques for state-of-charge estimation in battery systems, including open-circuit voltage (OCV) methods, Kalman filtering, and neural networks.
    • State-of-Health (SOH) Monitoring: Methods for state-of-health monitoring in battery systems, including capacity estimation, impedance spectroscopy, and aging models.
    • Thermal Modeling and Management: Thermal modeling of battery systems, including heat generation, heat transfer, and thermal management strategies for temperature control and safety.
    • Cycling and Degradation Analysis: Analysis of battery cycling behavior, degradation mechanisms, and lifetime estimation using cycle life models and degradation models.
    • Performance Optimization: Techniques for performance optimization of battery systems, including charge-discharge optimization, energy management strategies, and peak shaving.
    • Model Validation and Verification: Validation and verification of battery models using experimental data, field tests, and benchmarking against real-world performance.
    • Simulation Tools and Software: Overview of simulation tools and software for battery modeling and simulation, including MATLAB/Simulink, COMSOL Multiphysics, and ANSYS Fluent.
    • Case Studies and Applications: Case studies and applications of battery modeling and simulation in electric vehicle design, energy management systems, and battery pack optimization.
    • Solid-State Batteries: Exploration of solid-state battery technology, including solid electrolytes, lithium metal anodes, and ceramic separators, offering higher energy density, safety, and longevity.
    • Lithium-Sulfur Batteries: Overview of lithium-sulfur battery chemistry, materials, and challenges, including sulfur cathodes, lithium metal anodes, and polysulfide shuttling.
    • Lithium-Air Batteries: Discussion of lithium-air battery technology, including oxygen cathodes, lithium metal anodes, and electrolyte compositions, enabling high energy density and lightweight design.
    • Next-Generation Chemistries: Introduction to emerging battery chemistries and materials, including lithium-silicon, lithium-graphene, sodium-ion, and potassium-ion batteries, with potential applications in electric vehicles.
    • Advanced Manufacturing Techniques: Exploration of advanced manufacturing techniques for battery fabrication, including roll-to-roll processing, additive manufacturing, and 3D printing.
    • Energy Storage Systems Integration: Integration of advanced battery technologies into energy storage systems for electric vehicles, including battery pack design, thermal management, and safety features.
    • Materials Characterization and Testing: Characterization techniques for battery materials, including microscopy, spectroscopy, and electrochemical testing, to understand structure-property relationships and performance.
    • Performance Evaluation and Optimization: Evaluation and optimization of advanced battery technologies for electric vehicle applications, considering factors such as energy density, power density, cycle life, and cost.
    • Regulatory and Safety Considerations: Considerations for regulatory compliance and safety testing of advanced battery technologies, including UN38.3 certification, UL standards, and safety protocols.
    • Future Directions and Challenges: Discussion of future directions and challenges in advanced battery technology research and development, including scale-up, cost reduction, and commercialization.
    • Project Selection: Choose from a range of project topics related to battery technology for electric vehicles, including battery pack design, performance optimization, thermal management, and safety analysis.
    • Project Planning and Management: Develop project plans, schedules, and budgets, and manage resources effectively to ensure timely delivery of project milestones.
    • System Design and Implementation: Design and implement battery systems solutions, including battery pack layout, cell selection, thermal control systems, and safety features.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of battery systems solutions, including performance testing, safety testing, and environmental testing.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the project development process, outcomes, and lessons learned, and prepare technical reports, presentations, and demonstrations for stakeholders.
    • Collaboration and Teamwork: Collaborate with team members, stakeholders, and industry partners to leverage collective expertise and resources, and achieve project objectives effectively.
    • Project Showcase: Showcase completed projects through presentations, demonstrations, and exhibitions, highlighting innovative features, technical achievements, and potential applications.
    • Feedback and Iteration: Collect feedback from users, stakeholders, and mentors, and iterate on project designs and implementations to improve functionality, usability, and performance.
    • Professional Development: Reflect on the project experience and identify opportunities for professional growth and skill enhancement in battery technology for electric vehicles.
    • Career Pathways: Explore career pathways and opportunities in battery technology research, development, testing, and manufacturing for electric vehicles.
