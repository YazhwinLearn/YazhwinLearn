    Overview Control of Electric Vehicle Course

    Control of electric vehicles (EVs) involves designing and implementing control algorithms to regulate vehicle dynamics, energy management, and power distribution in electric propulsion systems. This course covers topics such as traction control, regenerative braking, torque vectoring, and vehicle stability control. Students will learn how to optimize the performance, efficiency, and safety of electric vehicles through advanced control strategies.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of Electric Vehicle Propulsion Systems: Introduction to electric vehicle architectures, including battery electric vehicles (BEVs), hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs), and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs).
    • Electric Drive Components: Understanding the components of electric drive systems, including electric motors, power electronics, and energy storage systems (batteries or ultracapacitors).
    • Powertrain Control Strategies: Explore control strategies for electric vehicle powertrains, including torque control, speed control, and regenerative braking.
    • Vehicle Dynamics and Stability: Considerations for vehicle dynamics and stability control in electric vehicles, including traction control, stability control, and torque vectoring.
    • Energy Management Systems: Introduction to energy management systems (EMS) for electric vehicles, including battery state-of-charge (SOC) estimation, power distribution, and energy optimization.
    • Thermal Management Systems: Understanding thermal management systems for electric vehicles, including cooling systems for electric motors, power electronics, and battery packs.
    • Human-Machine Interface (HMI): Design principles for human-machine interface (HMI) in electric vehicles, including displays, controls, and driver assistance systems.
    • Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) Integration: Overview of vehicle-to-grid (V2G) integration technologies, enabling bidirectional energy flow between electric vehicles and the electric grid.
    • Charging Control and Infrastructure: Considerations for electric vehicle charging control and infrastructure, including charging protocols, standards, and smart charging technologies.
    • Regulatory Compliance and Safety: Understanding regulatory requirements and safety standards for electric vehicle control systems, including ISO standards, SAE standards, and NHTSA regulations.
    • Electric Motor Control: Techniques for electric motor control in electric vehicles, including field-oriented control (FOC), direct torque control (DTC), and pulse-width modulation (PWM) techniques.
    • Power Electronics Control: Understanding control strategies for power electronics converters, including inverters, converters, and DC-DC converters, for efficient power transfer and conversion.
    • Battery Management Systems (BMS): Overview of battery management systems (BMS) control algorithms for electric vehicle batteries, including state-of-charge (SOC) estimation, balancing, and thermal management.
    • Energy Optimization Algorithms: Exploration of energy optimization algorithms for electric vehicles, including route planning, speed optimization, and energy recuperation strategies.
    • Regenerative Braking Control: Techniques for regenerative braking control in electric vehicles, including brake blending, torque blending, and energy recovery algorithms.
    • Dynamic Performance Control: Considerations for dynamic performance control in electric vehicles, including torque vectoring, traction control, and stability control algorithms.
    • Adaptive Control Strategies: Introduction to adaptive control strategies for electric vehicle powertrains, enabling adaptation to varying operating conditions, terrain, and driver preferences.
    • Model Predictive Control (MPC): Overview of model predictive control (MPC) techniques for electric vehicle powertrains, optimizing control actions based on predictive models and performance objectives.
    • Real-Time Implementation: Techniques for real-time implementation of control algorithms in electric vehicle powertrain systems, considering computational efficiency, latency, and hardware constraints.
    • Case Studies and Applications: Case studies and applications of advanced control strategies in electric vehicle powertrains, including performance validation, testing, and optimization.
    • Autonomous Vehicle Architectures: Overview of autonomous vehicle architectures, including sensor suites, perception systems, computing platforms, and control interfaces.
    • Perception and Localization: Understanding perception and localization systems for autonomous electric vehicles, including sensor fusion, simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM), and object detection algorithms.
    • Decision-Making Algorithms: Exploration of decision-making algorithms for autonomous electric vehicles, including path planning, behavior prediction, and traffic negotiation strategies.
    • Human-Machine Interaction (HMI): Design principles for human-machine interaction (HMI) in autonomous electric vehicles, including user interfaces, voice commands, and gesture recognition systems.
    • Safety and Redundancy: Considerations for safety and redundancy in autonomous electric vehicle control systems, including fail-safe mechanisms, fault detection and isolation (FDI), and backup systems.
    • Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) Communication: Overview of vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication technologies for autonomous electric vehicles, enabling cooperative sensing and decision-making.
    • Legal and Ethical Considerations: Understanding legal and ethical considerations in autonomous electric vehicle control, including liability, privacy, and regulatory compliance.
    • Real-Time Control and Adaptation: Techniques for real-time control and adaptation in autonomous electric vehicle systems, enabling response to changing environmental conditions, traffic scenarios, and user preferences.
    • Integration with Smart Infrastructure: Considerations for integrating autonomous electric vehicles with smart infrastructure, including traffic management systems, road infrastructure, and vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) communication.
    • Case Studies and Applications: Case studies and applications of autonomous electric vehicle control technologies, including autonomous taxis, delivery vehicles, and urban mobility solutions.
    • Project Selection: Choose from a range of project topics related to electric vehicle control systems, including powertrain optimization, energy management, autonomous driving algorithms, and human-machine interface design.
    • Project Planning and Management: Develop project plans, schedules, and budgets, and manage resources effectively to ensure timely delivery of project milestones.
    • System Design and Implementation: Design and implement control systems solutions for electric vehicles, including hardware and software components, algorithm development, and integration with vehicle platforms.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of control systems solutions for electric vehicles, including simulation-based testing, hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) testing, and real-world validation.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the project development process, outcomes, and lessons learned, and prepare technical reports, presentations, and demonstrations for stakeholders.
    • Collaboration and Teamwork: Collaborate with team members, stakeholders, and industry partners to leverage collective expertise and resources, and achieve project objectives effectively.
    • Project Showcase: Showcase completed projects through presentations, demonstrations, and exhibitions, highlighting innovative features, technical achievements, and potential applications.
    • Feedback and Iteration: Collect feedback from users, stakeholders, and mentors, and iterate on project designs and implementations to improve functionality, usability, and performance.
    • Professional Development: Reflect on the project experience and identify opportunities for professional growth and skill enhancement in electric vehicle control systems.
    • Career Pathways: Explore career pathways and opportunities in electric vehicle control systems development, testing, validation, and implementation.
