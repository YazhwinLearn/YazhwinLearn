    Overview Embedded Systems for Electric Vehicle Course

    Embedded systems for electric vehicles (EVs) focuses on the design and development of embedded systems used in electric and hybrid electric vehicles. This course covers topics such as powertrain control, battery management, motor control, and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) communication. Students will learn how to program microcontrollers, interface sensors and actuators, and implement control algorithms for EV applications.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of Electric Vehicles: Introduction to electric vehicle (EV) technology, including types of EVs, electric drivetrains, and battery systems.
    • Role of Embedded Systems: Understand the role of embedded systems in electric vehicles, including control, monitoring, and communication functions.
    • Embedded System Architecture: Exploration of embedded system architectures for electric vehicles, including hardware components, sensors, actuators, and communication interfaces.
    • Microcontroller Platforms: Overview of microcontroller platforms used in automotive embedded systems, including ARM Cortex-M, Renesas RH850, and Infineon AURIX.
    • Embedded Software Development: Introduction to embedded software development tools, languages, and methodologies for programming automotive embedded systems.
    • Electric Vehicle Control Strategies: Understanding control strategies for electric vehicle propulsion, including motor control, battery management, and regenerative braking.
    • Diagnostic and Monitoring Systems: Explore diagnostic and monitoring systems for electric vehicles, including fault detection, state estimation, and onboard diagnostics (OBD).
    • Embedded Communication Protocols: Overview of communication protocols used in automotive embedded systems, including CAN, LIN, FlexRay, and Ethernet.
    • Human-Machine Interface (HMI): Considerations for designing human-machine interfaces in electric vehicles, including displays, controls, and driver assistance systems.
    • Integration with Vehicle Networks: Understanding integration with vehicle networks and systems, including body control, chassis control, and infotainment systems.
    • Real-Time Operating Systems (RTOS): Introduction to real-time operating systems used in automotive embedded systems for deterministic behavior and task scheduling.
    • Advanced Control Algorithms: Explore advanced control algorithms for electric vehicle propulsion and energy management, including model predictive control (MPC) and adaptive control.
    • Cybersecurity in Automotive Systems: Understanding cybersecurity threats and countermeasures in automotive embedded systems, including secure communication, encryption, and intrusion detection.
    • Functional Safety Standards: Overview of functional safety standards for automotive embedded systems, including ISO 26262, and safety integrity levels (SIL).
    • Autonomous Driving Systems: Introduction to autonomous driving systems and embedded technologies, including sensor fusion, perception algorithms, and decision-making systems.
    • Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) Communication: Explore V2X communication technologies for vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V), vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I), and vehicle-to-cloud (V2C) communication.
    • Embedded Systems Testing and Validation: Techniques for testing and validation of embedded systems in electric vehicles, including simulation, hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) testing, and field testing.
    • Energy Efficiency Optimization: Considerations for optimizing energy efficiency in electric vehicles through embedded system design, including power management, thermal management, and energy recovery systems.
    • Wireless Charging Systems: Introduction to wireless charging systems for electric vehicles and embedded control algorithms for efficient and safe charging operations.
    • Emerging Trends and Technologies: Discussion of emerging trends and technologies in embedded systems design for electric vehicles, including AI-powered systems, edge computing, and predictive maintenance.
    • Project Selection: Choose from a range of project topics related to embedded systems design for electric vehicles, including motor control, battery management, cybersecurity, and autonomous driving.
    • Project Planning and Management: Develop project plans, schedules, and budgets, and manage resources effectively to ensure timely delivery of project milestones.
    • System Design and Implementation: Design and implement embedded systems solutions for specific applications in electric vehicles, considering hardware and software components, control algorithms, and communication protocols.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of embedded systems solutions, including functional testing, performance evaluation, and safety testing.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the project development process, outcomes, and lessons learned, and prepare technical reports, presentations, and demonstrations for stakeholders.
    • Collaboration and Teamwork: Collaborate with team members, stakeholders, and industry partners to leverage collective expertise and resources, and achieve project objectives effectively.
    • Project Showcase: Showcase completed projects through presentations, demonstrations, and exhibitions, highlighting innovative features, technical achievements, and potential applications.
    • Feedback and Iteration: Collect feedback from users, stakeholders, and mentors, and iterate on project designs and implementations to improve functionality, usability, and performance.
    • Professional Development: Reflect on the project experience and identify opportunities for professional growth and skill enhancement in embedded systems design and automotive technology.
    • Career Pathways: Explore career pathways and opportunities in embedded systems design, automotive engineering, and related fields, including research, development, and application.
