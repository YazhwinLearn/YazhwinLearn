    Overview Advanced PCB Design Course

    Advanced PCB design covers advanced techniques and methodologies for designing printed circuit boards (PCBs) with high-speed digital, analog, and mixed-signal components. This course explores topics such as signal integrity, power integrity, thermal management, and EMI/EMC considerations. Students will learn how to design complex PCB layouts using industry-standard CAD software and optimize board designs for manufacturability and performance.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of PCB Design Process: Introduction to the PCB design process, including schematic capture, component placement, routing, and manufacturing.
    • PCB Design Tools and Software: Overview of PCB design tools and software suites, such as Altium Designer, Cadence Allegro, and Mentor Graphics PADS, and their features.
    • Design Guidelines and Standards: Understand design guidelines and industry standards for PCB layout, including signal integrity, power distribution, thermal management, and electromagnetic compatibility (EMC).
    • Component Selection and Footprint Design: Considerations for selecting electronic components and creating custom component footprints for PCB layout, including package types, land patterns, and footprint libraries.
    • Schematic Capture and Netlist Generation: Learn how to create schematics and generate netlists for PCB design, ensuring accurate representation of circuit connections and components.
    • PCB Layout and Routing Techniques: Explore PCB layout and routing techniques, including trace routing, via placement, layer stackup, and impedance control, to meet design requirements and performance goals.
    • High-Speed PCB Design: Understanding the challenges and best practices for high-speed PCB design, including signal integrity analysis, transmission line effects, and impedance matching.
    • Power Distribution and Grounding: Design considerations for power distribution networks (PDN) and grounding systems in PCB layout, including power plane design, decoupling capacitors, and ground loops.
    • EMC and EMI Mitigation: Techniques for mitigating electromagnetic interference (EMI) and ensuring electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) in PCB designs, including shielding, filtering, and layout optimization.
    • Thermal Management: Strategies for thermal management in PCB layout, including heatsink placement, thermal vias, and thermal relief patterns, to dissipate heat and prevent overheating of components.
    • Advanced Routing Strategies: Explore advanced routing strategies and automation techniques in PCB layout, including differential pairs, matched lengths, routing topologies, and design rule checks (DRC).
    • Flex and Rigid-Flex PCB Design: Introduction to flex and rigid-flex PCB design, including material selection, stackup design, bend radius considerations, and flex-to-board connections.
    • High-Density Interconnect (HDI) PCBs: Understanding the design considerations and manufacturing challenges of high-density interconnect (HDI) PCBs, including microvias, blind vias, and buried vias.
    • Multi-Layer PCB Design: Techniques for multi-layer PCB design, including layer stackup planning, signal routing strategies, and impedance matching for complex electronic systems.
    • Signal Integrity Analysis: Introduction to signal integrity (SI) analysis tools and methodologies for assessing signal integrity issues, including reflections, crosstalk, and timing violations, and optimizing PCB designs for high-speed signaling.
    • Power Integrity Analysis: Understanding power integrity (PI) analysis techniques for analyzing power distribution networks (PDN), voltage regulation, and transient response in PCB designs.
    • Design for Manufacturing (DFM): Considerations for designing PCBs for manufacturability, including panelization, assembly constraints, design for test (DFT), and design for assembly (DFA).
    • Design for Assembly (DFA): Strategies for optimizing PCB designs for ease of assembly and solderability, including component placement, solder mask design, and assembly process compatibility.
    • Design for Test (DFT): Techniques for designing PCBs for ease of testing and debugging, including test point placement, boundary scan testing, and built-in self-test (BIST) features.
    • Reliability and Quality Assurance: Understanding reliability considerations and quality assurance measures in PCB design, including design reviews, design verification testing (DVT), and failure analysis.
    • Project Selection: Choose from a range of project topics related to advanced PCB design, including high-speed digital systems, RF/microwave circuits, power electronics, and IoT devices.
    • Project Planning and Management: Develop project plans, schedules, and budgets, and manage resources effectively to ensure timely delivery of project milestones.
    • PCB Design and Layout: Design and layout PCBs for specific applications, considering advanced design techniques, signal integrity, power integrity, thermal management, and manufacturability.
    • Simulation and Analysis: Conduct simulation and analysis of PCB designs using industry-standard tools, such as SPICE simulators, signal integrity analysis software, and thermal analysis tools.
    • Prototype Fabrication: Fabricate prototypes of PCB designs using rapid prototyping techniques or outsourcing to fabrication houses, ensuring compliance with design specifications and quality standards.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of PCB prototypes, including functional testing, electrical testing, signal integrity testing, and reliability testing.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the project development process, outcomes, and lessons learned, and prepare technical reports, presentations, and documentation for stakeholders.
    • Collaboration and Teamwork: Collaborate with team members, stakeholders, and industry partners to leverage collective expertise and resources, and achieve project objectives effectively.
    • Project Showcase: Showcase completed projects through presentations, demonstrations, and exhibitions, highlighting innovative features, technical achievements, and potential applications.
    • Feedback and Iteration: Collect feedback from users, stakeholders, and mentors, and iterate on project designs and implementations to improve functionality, usability, and performance.
    • Professional Development: Reflect on the project experience and identify opportunities for professional growth and skill enhancement in advanced PCB design and electronic systems engineering.
