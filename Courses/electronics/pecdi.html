    Overview Design Concepts of Power Electronic Converters for Industries Course

    Design concepts of power electronic converters for industries explores the principles, design methodologies, and applications of power electronic converters in industrial systems. This course covers topics such as AC-DC converters, DC-DC converters, inverters, and motor drives. Students will learn how to design and analyze power electronic circuits for various industrial applications, including renewable energy systems, motor drives, and power supplies.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of Power Electronics: Introduction to power electronics and its significance in modern industrial applications, including energy conversion, motor control, and power conditioning.
    • Basics of Power Semiconductor Devices: Understanding the characteristics and operating principles of power semiconductor devices, including diodes, transistors, and thyristors.
    • Switching Converter Topologies: Exploration of switching converter topologies, such as buck, boost, buck-boost, and flyback converters, and their applications in voltage regulation and power conversion.
    • AC-DC Converters: Analysis and design of AC-DC converters for rectification, power factor correction (PFC), and DC voltage regulation in industrial power supplies and motor drives.
    • DC-DC Converters: Design considerations for DC-DC converters, including voltage step-up, step-down, and isolation, for efficient power transfer and voltage regulation in various industrial systems.
    • DC-AC Inverters: Understanding DC-AC inverters for converting DC power sources into AC waveforms suitable for motor drives, grid-tie applications, uninterruptible power supplies (UPS), and renewable energy systems.
    • Resonant Converters: Overview of resonant converter topologies, such as LLC resonant converters, for achieving high efficiency, low EMI, and soft-switching operation in power electronic systems.
    • Multi-Level Converters: Introduction to multi-level converter topologies, including diode-clamped, flying capacitor, and cascaded H-bridge converters, for high-voltage applications and grid integration of renewable energy sources.
    • Gate Drive Circuits and Protection: Design of gate drive circuits and protection mechanisms for power semiconductor devices, ensuring reliable and safe operation of power electronic converters.
    • Control Techniques: Techniques for control and modulation of power electronic converters, including pulse-width modulation (PWM), phase-shift modulation, and hysteresis control, for achieving desired output waveforms and performance characteristics.
    • Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs): Understanding the operation and control of VFDs for variable-speed motor control in industrial automation, HVAC systems, and pump applications.
    • Renewable Energy Systems: Analysis and design of power electronic converters for grid-tie inverters, solar photovoltaic (PV) systems, wind turbines, and energy storage systems in renewable energy applications.
    • Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS): Overview of UPS systems and their components, including rectifiers, inverters, and energy storage devices, for providing backup power and voltage regulation in critical industrial processes.
    • Active Power Filters: Understanding the role of active power filters in harmonic mitigation, power factor correction (PFC), and voltage regulation in industrial power distribution systems.
    • Switched-Mode Power Supplies (SMPS): Analysis and design of SMPS architectures, including forward, flyback, and push-pull converters, for efficient AC-DC and DC-DC power conversion in industrial equipment and appliances.
    • Grid Integration of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs): Techniques for interfacing renewable energy sources, energy storage systems, and microgrids with the utility grid using power electronic converters, enabling grid stability, power quality improvement, and demand response.
    • Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure: Design considerations for power electronic converters in electric vehicle (EV) charging stations, including AC chargers, DC fast chargers, and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) systems, for efficient and reliable charging of electric vehicles.
    • Industrial Automation and Robotics: Applications of power electronic converters in industrial automation and robotics systems, including servo drives, motion control systems, and robotic manipulators, for precise control and energy efficiency.
    • Energy Management Systems: Overview of energy management systems (EMS) and supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems for monitoring, control, and optimization of power electronic converters and industrial energy assets.
    • Smart Grid Technologies: Introduction to smart grid technologies, including advanced metering infrastructure (AMI), demand response (DR), and distributed energy resources management systems (DERMS), leveraging power electronic converters for grid modernization and optimization.
    • Project Selection: Choose from a range of project topics related to power electronic converter design, including motor drives, renewable energy systems, power supplies, and grid integration solutions.
    • Project Planning and Management: Define project objectives, scope, and deliverables, and develop a project plan outlining key milestones, tasks, and resource requirements.
    • System Specification and Requirements: Gather and analyze system requirements, specifications, and constraints for the power electronic converter design project, considering factors such as voltage, current, power rating, efficiency, and reliability.
    • Conceptual Design and Simulation: Develop conceptual designs and conduct simulation studies using software tools such as MATLAB/Simulink, PLECS, or LTspice to evaluate the performance and feasibility of different converter topologies and control strategies.
    • Hardware Implementation: Design and prototype the power electronic converter hardware, selecting appropriate components, layout, and packaging to meet the project requirements and performance targets.
    • Control Algorithm Development: Implement control algorithms and modulation techniques for the power electronic converter, optimizing performance parameters such as efficiency, power factor, and harmonic distortion.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of the power electronic converter prototype, including functional testing, performance characterization, and reliability analysis, to ensure compliance with design specifications and standards.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the design process, analysis results, and testing procedures in a comprehensive project report, and prepare presentations to communicate the project outcomes and findings to stakeholders and peers.
    • Project Presentation and Demonstration: Present the power electronic converter design project to an audience of industry professionals, academics, and peers, showcasing the innovation, technical excellence, and practical relevance of the project.
    • Project Evaluation and Reflection: Reflect on the project experience, evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the design solution, and identify opportunities for future improvement and innovation in power electronic converter design.
    • Professional Development: Demonstrate your project experience and expertise in power electronic converter design through your portfolio, resume, and professional networking channels, positioning yourself for career opportunities in industrial automation, power systems, renewable energy, and related fields.
