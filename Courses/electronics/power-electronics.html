    Overview Power Electronics Design Course

    Power electronics design covers the design and implementation of electronic circuits and systems for power conversion, control, and conditioning. This course explores topics such as power semiconductor devices, converter topologies, modulation techniques, and circuit simulation. Students will learn how to design power electronic systems for applications ranging from renewable energy systems to electric vehicles and industrial automation.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Overview of Power Electronics: Introduction to power electronics and its significance in modern electrical systems, including renewable energy, electric vehicles, industrial automation, and consumer electronics.
    • Power Semiconductor Devices: Understanding the characteristics, operation, and applications of power semiconductor devices, such as diodes, MOSFETs, IGBTs, and thyristors, in power electronic circuits.
    • Switched-Mode Power Supplies (SMPS): Analysis and design of SMPS architectures, including buck, boost, buck-boost, and flyback converters, for efficient AC-DC and DC-DC power conversion in various applications.
    • Motor Drives and Control: Exploration of motor drive systems, including DC motor drives, induction motor drives, and synchronous motor drives, and their control strategies for speed and torque regulation.
    • Power Factor Correction (PFC): Overview of power factor correction techniques, including active PFC and passive PFC, for improving power quality and efficiency in AC-DC power supplies and motor drives.
    • Resonant Converters: Analysis and design of resonant converter topologies, such as LLC resonant converters and phase-shifted full-bridge converters, for high-frequency, high-efficiency power conversion.
    • Grid-Tie Inverters: Understanding the operation and control of grid-tie inverters for photovoltaic (PV) systems, wind turbines, and energy storage systems, enabling renewable energy integration with the utility grid.
    • Electric Vehicle Power Electronics: Applications of power electronics in electric vehicle (EV) systems, including traction inverters, battery chargers, and DC-DC converters, for efficient energy conversion and management.
    • Power Supplies and Converters: Design considerations for AC-DC and DC-DC power supplies, including linear regulators, switching regulators, and isolated converters, for various consumer, industrial, and automotive applications.
    • Control Techniques and Modulation Strategies: Understanding control techniques and modulation strategies for power electronic converters, including pulse-width modulation (PWM), space vector modulation (SVM), and direct torque control (DTC).
    • Wide-Bandgap Semiconductor Devices: Introduction to wide-bandgap (WBG) semiconductor materials, such as silicon carbide (SiC) and gallium nitride (GaN), and their advantages in high-power, high-frequency power electronic applications.
    • Multi-Level Converters: Analysis and design of multi-level converter topologies, including cascaded H-bridge, flying capacitor, and neutral-point clamped (NPC) converters, for medium-voltage, high-power applications in renewable energy and industrial drives.
    • Soft-Switching Techniques: Understanding soft-switching techniques, such as zero-voltage switching (ZVS) and zero-current switching (ZCS), for reducing switching losses and improving efficiency in power electronic converters.
    • Advanced Control Algorithms: Exploration of advanced control algorithms, such as predictive control, model predictive control (MPC), and adaptive control, for enhancing the performance, stability, and dynamic response of power electronic systems.
    • High-Frequency Power Magnetics: Design considerations for high-frequency power magnetics, including transformers, inductors, and magnetic materials, for minimizing size, weight, and losses in switched-mode power supplies and motor drives.
    • Integration of Energy Storage Systems: Applications of power electronics in energy storage systems, such as battery energy storage systems (BESS), supercapacitors, and flywheel energy storage, for grid stabilization, peak shaving, and renewable energy integration.
    • Advanced Thermal Management: Techniques for thermal management in power electronic systems, including heat sinks, thermal interface materials (TIMs), and liquid cooling solutions, for maintaining optimal operating temperatures and reliability.
    • Smart Grid Technologies: Overview of smart grid technologies, including demand response (DR), microgrids, and energy management systems (EMS), leveraging power electronics for grid modernization, efficiency improvement, and renewable energy integration.
    • Wireless Power Transfer (WPT): Introduction to wireless power transfer technologies, such as inductive coupling, resonant inductive coupling, and magnetic resonance, for wireless charging of electric vehicles, consumer electronics, and medical devices.
    • Power Electronics for Internet of Things (IoT): Applications of power electronics in IoT devices and systems, including energy harvesting, power management, and wireless sensor networks, for enabling smart and connected solutions.
    • Project Definition and Planning: Define project objectives, scope, and deliverables, and develop a project plan outlining key milestones, tasks, and resource requirements.
    • System Specification and Requirements: Gather and analyze system requirements, specifications, and constraints for the power electronic design project, considering factors such as voltage, current, power rating, efficiency, and reliability.
    • Conceptual Design and Simulation: Develop conceptual designs and conduct simulation studies using software tools such as MATLAB/Simulink, PLECS, or LTspice to evaluate the performance and feasibility of different power electronic topologies and control strategies.
    • Hardware Implementation: Design and prototype the power electronic hardware, selecting appropriate components, layout, and packaging to meet the project requirements and performance targets.
    • Control Algorithm Development: Implement control algorithms and modulation techniques for the power electronic system, optimizing performance parameters such as efficiency, power factor, and harmonic distortion.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct comprehensive testing and validation of the power electronic prototype, including functional testing, performance characterization, and reliability analysis, to ensure compliance with design specifications and standards.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the design process, analysis results, and testing procedures in a comprehensive project report, and prepare presentations to communicate the project outcomes and findings to stakeholders and peers.
    • Project Presentation and Demonstration: Present the power electronic design project to an audience of industry professionals, academics, and peers, showcasing the innovation, technical excellence, and practical relevance of the project.
    • Project Evaluation and Reflection: Reflect on the project experience, evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the design solution, and identify opportunities for future improvement and innovation in power electronics design.
    • Professional Development: Demonstrate your project experience and expertise in power electronics design through your portfolio, resume, and professional networking channels, positioning yourself for career opportunities in power systems, renewable energy, electric vehicles, and related fields.
