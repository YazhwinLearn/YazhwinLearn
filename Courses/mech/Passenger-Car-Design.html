    Overview Complete Passenger Car Design & Product Development Course

    Complete passenger car design and product development encompasses the entire process of designing, engineering, and manufacturing passenger vehicles from concept to production. This course provides a comprehensive overview of automotive design principles, vehicle dynamics, safety regulations, and manufacturing processes. Students will learn how to develop innovative and market-ready passenger car designs that meet performance, safety, and sustainability requirements.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Vehicle Architecture: Understand the basic components and systems of a passenger car, including chassis, powertrain, suspension, and body structure.
    • Aerodynamics: Learn about the principles of aerodynamics and their application in vehicle design to improve fuel efficiency, stability, and performance.
    • Safety Systems: Explore safety features and systems in passenger cars, including passive safety (crumple zones, airbags) and active safety (ABS, ESC, collision avoidance).
    • Interior Design: Understand interior layout, ergonomics, and comfort features in passenger cars, including seating, dashboard layout, infotainment systems, and connectivity.
    • Exterior Design: Learn about exterior styling, design language, and aesthetics in passenger car design, including body shapes, character lines, and lighting design.
    • Materials and Manufacturing: Explore materials selection, manufacturing processes, and assembly techniques used in passenger car production, including steel, aluminum, plastics, and composites.
    • Regulatory Requirements: Understand regulatory requirements and standards governing passenger car design, including crash safety, emissions, fuel economy, and noise regulations.
    • Market Trends and Consumer Preferences: Analyze market trends, consumer preferences, and demographic factors influencing passenger car design, including lifestyle, urbanization, and sustainability.
    • Environmental Considerations: Discuss environmental considerations in passenger car design, including emissions reduction, energy efficiency, recyclability, and sustainability initiatives.
    • Future Mobility Trends: Explore emerging trends and technologies shaping the future of passenger car design, including electric vehicles, autonomous driving, connectivity, and shared mobility.
    • Concept Development: Understand the process of generating and evaluating vehicle concepts based on market requirements, consumer feedback, and technological feasibility.
    • Design Validation: Explore methods for validating vehicle designs through computer-aided engineering (CAE), simulation, and prototype testing to ensure performance, safety, and regulatory compliance.
    • Prototyping and Testing: Learn about prototyping techniques and testing procedures for passenger cars, including physical prototypes, virtual simulations, wind tunnel testing, and crash testing.
    • Component Integration: Understand the integration of various vehicle components and systems, including powertrain, chassis, body structure, electrical systems, and interior features.
    • Manufacturability and Cost Optimization: Explore strategies for optimizing vehicle design for manufacturability, cost-effectiveness, and scalability, including design for assembly (DFA) and design for manufacturing (DFM) principles.
    • Quality Assurance and Control: Discuss quality assurance processes and quality control measures implemented throughout the product development process to ensure reliability, durability, and customer satisfaction.
    • Supply Chain Management: Understand supply chain management principles and practices in automotive manufacturing, including sourcing, procurement, logistics, and supplier collaboration.
    • Production Planning and Launch: Learn about production planning activities, including facility layout, equipment selection, process optimization, and ramp-up to volume production.
    • Lifecycle Management: Explore lifecycle management strategies for passenger cars, including product updates, recalls, warranty management, and end-of-life disposal/recycling.
    • Continuous Improvement: Discuss continuous improvement initiatives and feedback mechanisms to incorporate lessons learned and drive ongoing innovation in passenger car design and product development.
    • Electric Propulsion Systems: Learn about electric vehicle (EV) propulsion systems, including battery technology, electric motors, power electronics, and charging infrastructure.
    • Autonomous Driving Technologies: Explore the principles and components of autonomous driving systems, including sensors, perception algorithms, decision-making algorithms, and vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication.
    • Connected Vehicle Technologies: Understand connected vehicle technologies, including telematics, vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) communication, in-vehicle connectivity, and over-the-air (OTA) updates.
    • Advanced Materials and Lightweighting: Explore advanced materials used in passenger car design, including high-strength steel, aluminum alloys, carbon fiber composites, and polymer-based materials, to reduce weight and improve fuel efficiency.
    • Energy Storage and Management: Learn about energy storage and management systems in electric and hybrid vehicles, including battery management systems (BMS), thermal management, and regenerative braking.
    • Driver Assistance Systems: Understand driver assistance systems, including adaptive cruise control, lane-keeping assist, automatic emergency braking, and parking assistance, to enhance safety and convenience.
    • Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) Communication: Explore V2X communication technologies, including vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V), vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I), and vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communication, enabling cooperative driving and traffic management.
    • Smart Interiors and Infotainment: Learn about smart interior features and infotainment systems in passenger cars, including digital cockpits, augmented reality (AR) displays, voice assistants, and personalized user experiences.
    • Environmental Sustainability: Discuss sustainability initiatives in passenger car design, including eco-friendly materials, energy-efficient systems, recyclability, and carbon footprint reduction.
    • Future Trends and Innovations: Explore emerging trends and innovations in passenger car design, such as shared mobility, on-demand services, subscription models, and mobility-as-a-service (MaaS) platforms.
    • Project Planning and Conceptualization: Define project goals, requirements, and constraints, and develop initial design concepts and sketches based on market analysis and user needs.
    • Concept Selection and Validation: Evaluate design concepts using criteria such as performance, safety, cost, and feasibility, and select the most promising concept for further development.
    • Detail Design and Engineering Analysis: Develop detailed design drawings, perform engineering analysis and simulations, and optimize the design for performance, efficiency, and manufacturability.
    • Prototype Development: Build physical prototypes of the vehicle components and systems, including chassis, body structure, powertrain, suspension, and interior features.
    • Testing and Validation: Conduct prototype testing and validation, including static and dynamic tests, crash tests, aerodynamic tests, and performance evaluations, to verify design integrity and performance.
    • Iterative Design and Optimization: Iterate on the design based on test results and feedback, making refinements and optimizations to improve performance, safety, and user experience.
    • Documentation and Presentation: Document the design process, methodologies, analysis results, and test findings in a comprehensive project report, and present the project outcomes to stakeholders and peers.
    • Project Management and Collaboration: Manage project resources, timelines, and tasks, and collaborate effectively with team members, suppliers, and stakeholders to achieve project objectives within budget and schedule constraints.
    • Reflection and Continuous Improvement: Reflect on the project experience, identify lessons learned, and opportunities for improvement in design methodologies, processes, and outcomes for future projects.
