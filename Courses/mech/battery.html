    Overview Advanced Battery Design Technology Course

    Advanced battery design technology explores the latest advancements in battery materials, cell chemistry, and energy storage technologies. This course covers topics such as lithium-ion batteries, solid-state batteries, battery management systems, and safety standards. Students will learn how to design and optimize battery systems for various applications, including electric vehicles, renewable energy storage, and portable electronics.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Fundamentals of Battery Technology: Introduction to the basic principles of electrochemistry, battery operation, and key performance parameters such as capacity, voltage, and energy density.
    • Battery Materials and Components: Overview of battery materials, including electrodes, electrolytes, separators, and current collectors, and their role in battery performance and durability.
    • Battery Chemistries: Explore different battery chemistries, such as lithium-ion, lithium-polymer, nickel-metal hydride, and solid-state batteries, and their advantages, limitations, and applications.
    • Battery Modeling and Simulation: Introduction to battery modeling and simulation techniques for predicting battery behavior, performance, and degradation under different operating conditions and usage scenarios.
    • Battery Manufacturing Processes: Understand the manufacturing processes involved in battery production, including electrode fabrication, cell assembly, electrolyte filling, and cell testing and characterization.
    • Battery Testing and Characterization: Techniques for battery testing and characterization, including capacity measurement, cycle life testing, impedance spectroscopy, and thermal analysis, to evaluate battery performance and reliability.
    • Battery Management Systems (BMS): Overview of battery management systems (BMS) for monitoring, controlling, and optimizing battery operation, including state-of-charge (SoC) estimation, cell balancing, and thermal management.
    • Battery Safety and Reliability: Considerations for battery safety and reliability, including risk assessment, failure modes analysis, safety protocols, and design strategies for preventing thermal runaway and catastrophic failures.
    • Advanced Battery Technologies: Emerging trends and advancements in battery technology, such as solid-state electrolytes, silicon anodes, lithium-sulfur batteries, and beyond lithium-ion chemistries.
    • Environmental and Sustainability Considerations: Discussion on environmental impact and sustainability aspects of battery manufacturing, recycling, and end-of-life disposal, including strategies for minimizing environmental footprint and promoting circular economy principles.
    • Nanomaterials for Battery Electrodes: Applications of nanomaterials, such as carbon nanotubes, graphene, and metal oxides, in battery electrode design for enhancing energy storage capacity, rate capability, and cycling stability.
    • Solid-State Batteries: Overview of solid-state battery technology, including solid electrolytes, solid-state interfaces, and fabrication techniques, for improving safety, energy density, and lifespan of rechargeable batteries.
    • Next-Generation Electrolytes: Exploration of advanced electrolyte formulations, such as ionic liquids, polymer electrolytes, and gel electrolytes, for enhancing battery performance, stability, and safety under extreme conditions.
    • High-Energy-Density Battery Systems: Development of high-energy-density battery systems, such as lithium-sulfur, lithium-air, and metal-air batteries, for applications requiring long-range electric vehicles, grid-scale energy storage, and aerospace propulsion.
    • Flexible and Wearable Batteries: Design and fabrication of flexible and wearable battery technologies, including thin-film batteries, printed batteries, and textile-based batteries, for integration into wearable electronics, medical devices, and smart textiles.
    • Battery Recycling and Circular Economy: Strategies for battery recycling and resource recovery, including material recovery processes, closed-loop recycling systems, and sustainable end-of-life management practices, to minimize waste and conserve valuable resources.
    • Multi-Scale Modeling and Simulation: Advanced modeling and simulation techniques, such as multi-scale modeling, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and machine learning, for predicting battery performance, degradation mechanisms, and aging behavior at various length and time scales.
    • Advanced Battery Diagnostics: Development of advanced diagnostic techniques, such as in-situ and operando characterization methods, for real-time monitoring of battery health, performance, and state-of-health (SoH) during operation.
    • Electrification and Energy Storage Integration: Integration of batteries into electrified transportation systems, renewable energy grids, and distributed energy storage networks, including challenges and opportunities for grid stability, peak shaving, and demand-side management.
    • Future Directions in Battery Research: Exploration of emerging research directions and disruptive innovations in battery technology, such as quantum batteries, biological batteries, and beyond lithium-ion energy storage concepts, for addressing future energy challenges and opportunities.
    • Project Definition and Scope: Define the objectives, scope, and deliverables of the battery design technology project, identifying specific engineering problems or design challenges to be addressed.
    • Conceptual Design and Ideation: Generate innovative design concepts and ideas for new battery technologies or improvements to existing battery systems, considering factors such as performance, safety, cost, and environmental impact.
    • Modeling and Simulation: Develop computational models and simulations to analyze battery performance, optimize design parameters, and predict behavior under different operating conditions and usage scenarios.
    • Prototype Development and Testing: Build prototypes of battery cells or systems based on the conceptual design, using appropriate materials, manufacturing processes, and testing procedures to evaluate performance and validate design assumptions.
    • Data Analysis and Optimization: Analyze experimental data and simulation results to identify opportunities for design optimization and performance improvement, iterating on the design based on feedback and validation results.
    • Performance Evaluation and Validation: Conduct comprehensive performance testing and validation of the developed battery prototypes, including electrical testing, cycling tests, and safety evaluations, to ensure compliance with design specifications and industry standards.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the project methodology, design process, experimental results, and findings in a comprehensive project report, including technical drawings, data analysis, and recommendations for future work.
    • Presentation and Demonstration: Present the battery design technology project to stakeholders, peers, and industry professionals, showcasing the problem-solving approach, methodology, results, and insights gained from the project.
    • Peer Review and Feedback: Receive feedback and constructive criticism from peers, mentors, and experts in the field, incorporating suggestions for improvement and refinement of the project work and presentation.
    • Reflection and Learning Outcomes: Reflect on the project experience, lessons learned, and challenges encountered during the battery design technology project, identifying areas of personal growth and future development in the field.
    • Professional Development and Networking: Showcase your project work, expertise, and achievements in battery design technology through your portfolio, resume, and professional networking channels, positioning yourself for career opportunities and further education in battery engineering and related disciplines.
