    Overview Advanced CAD Design Course

    Advanced computer-aided design (CAD) covers advanced techniques and tools for creating, analyzing, and optimizing 3D models and engineering designs. This course dives into topics such as parametric modeling, surface modeling, assembly design, and finite element analysis (FEA). Students will learn how to use CAD software effectively to develop complex and innovative product designs that meet functional and manufacturing requirements.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Fundamentals of CAD: Introduction to computer-aided design (CAD) principles, workflows, and applications in engineering, architecture, and product design.
    • Parametric Modeling: Understanding parametric modeling techniques for creating 3D models with adjustable parameters, constraints, and relationships.
    • Assembly Design: Explore assembly design methodologies for creating complex assemblies, managing components, and testing motion and interference.
    • Surface Modeling: Introduction to surface modeling techniques for designing complex shapes, curves, and freeform surfaces in CAD software.
    • Sheet Metal Design: Overview of sheet metal design tools and workflows for creating sheet metal components, bends, flanges, and flat patterns.
    • Part Design: Understanding part design features and operations for creating solid models, extrusions, revolutions, sweeps, and fillets.
    • Drafting and Annotation: Learn drafting standards, annotation tools, and dimensioning techniques for creating detailed engineering drawings and documentation.
    • Visualization and Rendering: Explore visualization and rendering techniques for creating realistic 3D renderings and presentations of CAD models.
    • Collaborative Design: Discuss collaborative design tools and workflows for sharing CAD data, managing revisions, and facilitating teamwork among designers and engineers.
    • Advanced CAD Features: Discover advanced features and functionalities of CAD software, such as advanced surface modeling, generative design, topology optimization, and additive manufacturing tools.
    • Generative Design and Optimization: Exploration of generative design algorithms and optimization techniques for automatically generating innovative design solutions based on user-defined constraints and objectives.
    • Topology Optimization: Introduction to topology optimization methods for optimizing the material distribution within a design space to achieve lightweight and structurally efficient components.
    • Simulation-Driven Design: Understanding simulation-driven design workflows for performing virtual testing, analysis, and validation of CAD models, improving product performance and reliability.
    • Design for Manufacturing (DFM): Learn design principles and guidelines for manufacturability, including considerations for machining, casting, injection molding, and additive manufacturing processes.
    • Advanced Surface Modeling: Delve deeper into advanced surface modeling techniques, such as spline modeling, NURBS surfaces, curvature analysis, and class-A surface design.
    • Parametric Design Automation: Explore parametric design automation tools and workflows for automating repetitive design tasks, creating configurable models, and optimizing design variants.
    • Integrated CAM: Overview of integrated computer-aided manufacturing (CAM) tools and workflows for generating toolpaths, machining instructions, and NC code directly from CAD models.
    • Virtual Prototyping and Digital Twin: Introduction to virtual prototyping and digital twin concepts, enabling designers to simulate, analyze, and optimize product performance throughout its lifecycle.
    • Cloud-Based CAD Collaboration: Discuss cloud-based CAD collaboration platforms and tools for real-time collaboration, version control, and project management in distributed design teams.
    • Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) in CAD: Explore the use of augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) technologies for immersive design visualization, simulation, and review.
    • Project Definition and Scope: Define the objectives, scope, and deliverables of the CAD design project, identifying specific design problems or challenges to be addressed.
    • Conceptual Design and Ideation: Generate innovative design concepts and ideas for addressing the project requirements, considering functional, aesthetic, and performance criteria.
    • 3D Modeling and Simulation: Develop detailed 3D models of the design concepts using CAD software, applying appropriate modeling techniques and features to achieve design intent.
    • Engineering Analysis and Validation: Perform engineering analysis and simulation to evaluate the performance, structural integrity, and manufacturability of the design concepts, optimizing design parameters and configurations as needed.
    • Design Optimization and Iteration: Iterate on the design concepts based on analysis results and stakeholder feedback, refining the design to meet project requirements and constraints.
    • Documentation and Drawing Production: Create detailed engineering drawings, documentation, and technical specifications for the final design, ensuring clarity, accuracy, and compliance with industry standards.
    • Prototype Development and Testing: Build physical prototypes or virtual prototypes of the final design, conducting testing, validation, and verification to ensure functional performance and reliability.
    • Project Management and Collaboration: Manage project timelines, resources, and tasks effectively, collaborating with team members, stakeholders, and external partners to achieve project objectives.
    • Presentation and Communication: Present the CAD design project to stakeholders, peers, and industry professionals, effectively communicating the design process, methodology, results, and recommendations.
    • Portfolio Development: Document the CAD design project in your portfolio, showcasing your design process, problem-solving skills, and technical expertise to potential employers and clients.
    • Reflection and Learning Outcomes: Reflect on the CAD design project experience, identifying lessons learned, challenges encountered, and areas for personal and professional development in CAD design.
