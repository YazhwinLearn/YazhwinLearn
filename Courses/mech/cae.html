    Overview Advanced Computer-aided Engineering Course

    Advanced computer-aided engineering (CAE) focuses on utilizing simulation and analysis tools to optimize product designs and engineering processes. This course covers topics such as finite element analysis (FEA), computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and multibody dynamics (MBD). Students will learn how to perform virtual testing, optimization, and validation of engineering designs to improve performance, reliability, and efficiency.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Fundamentals of CAE: Introduction to computer-aided engineering (CAE) principles, workflows, and applications in engineering design, analysis, and optimization.
    • Finite Element Analysis (FEA): Understand finite element analysis techniques for simulating structural, thermal, fluid flow, and electromagnetic behavior of engineering components and systems.
    • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD): Explore computational fluid dynamics methods for analyzing fluid flow, heat transfer, and aerodynamic performance in engineering applications.
    • Multi-Body Dynamics (MBD): Introduction to multi-body dynamics simulation techniques for modeling and analyzing mechanical systems with interconnected moving parts and rigid bodies.
    • Optimization Techniques: Learn optimization algorithms and techniques for improving product performance, reducing material usage, and minimizing manufacturing costs through simulation-driven design.
    • Simulation Workflow: Understand the CAE simulation workflow, including model preparation, meshing, boundary conditions, solver setup, analysis, and post-processing.
    • Verification and Validation: Discuss methods for verifying and validating CAE models and simulations, ensuring accuracy, reliability, and confidence in simulation results.
    • Design Optimization: Explore design optimization methodologies, such as topology optimization, shape optimization, and parameter optimization, for improving product performance and efficiency.
    • Material Modeling and Properties: Understand material modeling techniques and properties for simulating the behavior of engineering materials under various loading and environmental conditions.
    • Advanced Analysis Capabilities: Discover advanced analysis capabilities in CAE software, including nonlinear analysis, dynamic analysis, fatigue analysis, and probabilistic analysis.
    • Nonlinear Analysis: Explore nonlinear analysis techniques for simulating material nonlinearities, large deformations, contact interactions, and transient phenomena in engineering systems.
    • Dynamic Analysis: Understand dynamic analysis methods for simulating vibrations, impacts, and transient responses in mechanical and structural systems subjected to time-varying loads.
    • Transient Thermal Analysis: Learn transient thermal analysis techniques for predicting temperature distributions, thermal gradients, and heat transfer rates in thermal management applications.
    • Electromagnetic Simulation: Introduction to electromagnetic simulation methods for analyzing electromagnetic fields, currents, and interactions in electronic and electrical systems.
    • Acoustic Simulation: Explore acoustic simulation techniques for predicting noise levels, sound propagation, and vibration responses in automotive, aerospace, and consumer electronics.
    • Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI): Understand fluid-structure interaction simulation methods for modeling interactions between fluids and solid structures, such as fluid-induced vibrations and fluid-elastic instability.
    • Composite Material Analysis: Learn composite material analysis techniques for simulating the behavior of composite structures, including laminate modeling, failure prediction, and durability analysis.
    • Modeling Complex Systems: Discuss methodologies for modeling complex systems and subsystems in CAE software, including hierarchical modeling, component-based modeling, and system integration.
    • Advanced Meshing Techniques: Explore advanced meshing techniques for generating high-quality meshes, including automatic mesh generation, adaptive meshing, and mesh refinement strategies.
    • High-Performance Computing (HPC): Understand the role of high-performance computing (HPC) in CAE simulations, including parallel processing, cloud computing, and GPU acceleration.
    • Project Definition and Scope: Define the objectives, scope, and deliverables of the CAE simulation project, identifying specific engineering problems or challenges to be addressed.
    • Modeling and Simulation Setup: Prepare the CAE model, geometry, materials, boundary conditions, and loads for the simulation, ensuring accuracy and relevance to the project requirements.
    • Meshing and Preprocessing: Generate a high-quality mesh for the CAE model, optimizing mesh density, element types, and mesh quality to achieve accurate and efficient simulation results.
    • Solver Setup and Analysis: Configure the solver settings, solution parameters, and analysis options for the simulation, running the analysis and monitoring convergence and stability.
    • Post-Processing and Results Analysis: Interpret and analyze simulation results, visualizing key engineering parameters, trends, and insights to draw meaningful conclusions and make informed decisions.
    • Validation and Verification: Validate simulation results against experimental data, analytical solutions, or empirical correlations, ensuring the accuracy and reliability of the simulation model.
    • Optimization and Sensitivity Analysis: Perform design optimization and sensitivity analysis to explore design alternatives, identify critical parameters, and optimize product performance and efficiency.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the CAE simulation project, including model setup, analysis methodology, results interpretation, and recommendations, in a comprehensive technical report.
    • Presentation and Communication: Present the CAE simulation project to stakeholders, peers, and industry professionals, effectively communicating the project objectives, methodology, findings, and conclusions.
    • Reflection and Learning Outcomes: Reflect on the CAE simulation project experience, identifying lessons learned, challenges encountered, and areas for further exploration and development in advanced CAE.
