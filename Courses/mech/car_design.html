    Overview

    Car Model Sketching and Designing is a specialized field within automotive design that focuses on the conceptualization and visualization of vehicles through sketches and digital renderings. It encompasses various aspects such as understanding proportions, lines, and form development specific to cars, as well as utilizing both traditional and digital tools for sketching and rendering. This discipline emphasizes creativity, attention to detail, and an understanding of automotive aesthetics and functionality. Car Model Sketching and Designing is essential in the automotive industry for generating ideas, exploring design concepts, and communicating design proposals visually. It plays a crucial role in the development of concept cars, production models, and prototypes, shaping the future of automotive design and innovation.

    Program Curriculum

    • History of Automotive Design: Explore the evolution of car design from its inception to modern-day trends, studying iconic designs and influential designers.
    • Principles of Car Design: Learn about the fundamental principles of automotive design, including proportion, balance, symmetry, and aerodynamics.
    • Design Language and Aesthetics: Understand the concept of design language in automotive styling, analyzing brand identities, design cues, and visual elements.
    • Form Development Techniques: Master the art of form development in car design, including sketching, rendering, and 3D modeling techniques for exploring shapes and volumes.
    • Freehand Sketching: Learn freehand sketching techniques to quickly capture design concepts and explore different viewpoints.
    • Perspective Drawing: Master the principles of perspective drawing to create dynamic sketches with accurate proportions and spatial relationships.
    • Rendering Techniques: Explore rendering techniques using various drawing tools and mediums to add depth, shading, and texture to your sketches.
    • Sketching from Observation: Practice sketching from observation to develop your visual perception and improve your ability to capture real-world objects and details.
    • Introduction to CAD Software: Familiarize yourself with industry-standard CAD software used in automotive design, such as Autodesk Alias, Rhino, or Blender.
    • Surface Modeling: Explore surface modeling techniques to create smooth, organic shapes and surfaces for your car designs.
    • Rendering and Visualization: Learn rendering and visualization techniques to create photorealistic images and presentations of your digital car models.
    • Prototyping and Iteration: Use digital modeling tools to prototype and iterate on your car designs, exploring different variations and refining your concepts.
    • Project Brief: Receive a design brief outlining the requirements, objectives, and constraints of the design project.
    • Concept Development: Brainstorm and develop design concepts based on the project brief, considering factors such as user needs, market trends, and brand identity.
    • Sketching and Ideation: Translate your design concepts into sketches and ideate on different design directions, exploring form, proportion, and detail.
    • Digital Modeling: Create digital models of your concept car designs using CAD software, refining the shapes, surfaces, and details.
    • Rendering and Presentation: Generate photorealistic renderings and presentations of your digital car models, showcasing your design vision and storytelling skills.
    • Critique and Feedback: Participate in design critiques and receive feedback from instructors and peers to refine and improve your designs.
    • Final Presentation: Present your final concept car designs to the class, articulating your design decisions, inspirations, and vision for the future of automotive design.
