    Overview Computational Fluid Dynamics Course

    Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) is a branch of fluid mechanics that utilizes numerical methods and algorithms to analyze and solve fluid flow problems. This course covers the principles of CFD, governing equations, numerical discretization techniques, and turbulence modeling. Students will learn how to use commercial CFD software to simulate and analyze fluid flows in various engineering applications, including aerospace, automotive, and environmental systems.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Fundamentals of Fluid Mechanics: Review the basic principles of fluid mechanics, including fluid properties, conservation laws, and fluid flow behavior.
    • Numerical Methods in CFD: Understand the numerical methods and discretization techniques used in CFD simulations, including finite difference, finite volume, and finite element methods.
    • Introduction to CFD Software: Explore popular CFD software packages and tools used for pre-processing, meshing, solving, and post-processing of fluid flow simulations.
    • Flow Regimes and Governing Equations: Learn about different flow regimes, such as laminar and turbulent flow, and the governing equations, including the Navier-Stokes equations and turbulence models.
    • Boundary Conditions and Meshing: Understand the importance of boundary conditions and mesh quality in CFD simulations, including boundary types, boundary layer resolution, and grid generation techniques.
    • Turbulence Modeling: Explore turbulence modeling techniques, including Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) models, large eddy simulation (LES), and direct numerical simulation (DNS).
    • Validation and Verification: Discuss methods for validating and verifying CFD simulations against experimental data, analytical solutions, and empirical correlations.
    • Heat Transfer Analysis: Learn about heat transfer analysis using CFD, including conduction, convection, and radiation heat transfer in various engineering applications.
    • Multiphase Flows: Understand the simulation of multiphase flows, including two-phase and three-phase flows, phase change phenomena, and interfacial dynamics.
    • Combustion Modeling: Explore combustion modeling techniques for simulating combustion processes, including combustion chemistry, reaction kinetics, and pollutant emissions.
    • High-Speed Flows: Understand the simulation of high-speed flows, including compressible flow phenomena, shock waves, and supersonic and hypersonic aerodynamics.
    • Turbomachinery Simulation: Explore CFD simulations of turbomachinery components, such as turbines, compressors, and pumps, including blade aerodynamics and performance optimization.
    • Aeroacoustics Analysis: Learn about aeroacoustics simulation techniques for predicting noise generation, propagation, and control in aerospace, automotive, and industrial applications.
    • Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI): Understand fluid-structure interaction simulation methods for modeling interactions between fluid flow and solid structures, such as fluid-induced vibrations and fluid-elastic instability.
    • Environmental Flows: Explore CFD simulations of environmental flows, including atmospheric dispersion modeling, pollutant transport, and natural ventilation in buildings and urban environments.
    • Renewable Energy Applications: Learn about CFD applications in renewable energy systems, such as wind turbines, hydroelectric dams, tidal turbines, and solar collectors.
    • Biofluid Mechanics: Understand the simulation of biofluid flows in biological systems, including blood flow in cardiovascular systems, airflow in respiratory systems, and fluid-structure interactions in biomechanics.
    • Multiphysics Simulations: Explore multiphysics simulation techniques, including fluid-structure interaction (FSI), fluid-particle interaction (FPI), and fluid-thermal interaction (FTI) coupling.
    • Advanced Meshing and Grid Generation: Discover advanced meshing techniques for complex geometries and flow domains, including unstructured meshing, boundary layer meshing, and mesh adaptation strategies.
    • High-Performance Computing (HPC) in CFD: Understand the role of high-performance computing (HPC) in accelerating CFD simulations, including parallel processing, distributed computing, and cloud computing.
    • Project Definition and Scope: Define the objectives, scope, and deliverables of the CFD simulation project, identifying specific fluid flow problems or phenomena to be analyzed.
    • Geometry Preparation and Meshing: Prepare the geometry and mesh for the CFD simulation, ensuring proper resolution of flow features, boundary layers, and geometric details.
    • Boundary Conditions and Solver Setup: Define the boundary conditions, initial conditions, and solver settings for the CFD simulation, configuring the simulation setup for accurate and efficient results.
    • Simulation Execution and Monitoring: Run the CFD simulation and monitor the convergence, stability, and accuracy of the solution, adjusting solver settings as needed to ensure robustness.
    • Post-Processing and Results Analysis: Interpret and analyze the CFD simulation results, visualizing flow patterns, pressure distributions, velocity profiles, and other relevant quantities to gain insights into fluid behavior.
    • Validation and Verification: Validate the CFD simulation results against experimental data, analytical solutions, or empirical correlations, assessing the accuracy and reliability of the simulation predictions.
    • Report Writing and Documentation: Document the CFD simulation process, methodology, results, and conclusions in a comprehensive technical report, following professional standards and guidelines.
    • Presentation and Communication: Present the CFD simulation project findings to stakeholders, peers, and industry professionals, effectively communicating the analysis methodology, results interpretation, and recommendations.
    • Reflection and Learning Outcomes: Reflect on the CFD simulation project experience, identifying lessons learned, challenges encountered, and areas for further exploration and development in computational fluid dynamics.
