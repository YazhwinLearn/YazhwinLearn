    Overview Hybrid Electric Vehicle Design and Analysis Course

    Hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) design and analysis involves integrating electric and internal combustion powertrains to optimize vehicle efficiency and reduce emissions. This course explores the principles of HEV propulsion systems, energy management strategies, and vehicle performance modeling. Students will learn how to design and analyze hybrid electric vehicles using simulation tools and evaluate their environmental and economic benefits.

    Technologies Covered

    The Program Curriculum Details

    • Introduction to HEV Technology: Understand the basic concepts and principles of hybrid electric vehicle technology, including hybrid powertrains, energy storage systems, and regenerative braking.
    • Types of Hybrid Vehicles: Explore different types of hybrid electric vehicles, including parallel hybrids, series hybrids, plug-in hybrids, and mild hybrids, and their respective advantages and limitations.
    • Hybrid Powertrain Architecture: Learn about the architecture and components of hybrid powertrains, including electric motors, internal combustion engines, transmissions, and power electronics.
    • Energy Storage Systems: Examine various energy storage systems used in hybrid electric vehicles, such as batteries, ultracapacitors, and flywheel energy storage, and their characteristics and performance metrics.
    • Regenerative Braking Systems: Understand regenerative braking systems in hybrid vehicles, including the principles of energy recovery, braking force distribution, and control strategies for maximizing energy efficiency.
    • Vehicle Electrification: Explore the process of electrifying conventional vehicles to create hybrid electric vehicles, including retrofitting existing vehicles and designing new hybrid platforms.
    • Hybrid Vehicle Control Strategies: Learn about control strategies for hybrid electric vehicle operation, including energy management, power distribution, and mode selection algorithms for optimizing fuel efficiency and performance.
    • Hybrid Vehicle Dynamics and Performance: Discuss the dynamic behavior and performance characteristics of hybrid electric vehicles, including acceleration, braking, handling, and energy consumption under different driving conditions.
    • Environmental and Economic Impacts: Consider the environmental and economic impacts of hybrid electric vehicles, including greenhouse gas emissions, air quality improvements, fuel savings, and total cost of ownership.
    • Future Trends and Developments: Explore emerging trends and advancements in hybrid electric vehicle technology, including advancements in battery technology, electrification strategies, and integration with renewable energy sources.
    • Vehicle Modeling and Simulation: Learn about modeling and simulation techniques for hybrid electric vehicles, including mathematical modeling of vehicle dynamics, powertrain components, and control systems.
    • Powertrain Optimization: Explore optimization methods for hybrid powertrain design and control, including optimization algorithms, sensitivity analysis, and trade-off studies for performance and efficiency.
    • Energy Management Strategies: Understand energy management strategies for hybrid electric vehicles, including rule-based, optimization-based, and predictive control methods for powertrain operation and energy distribution.
    • Component Sizing and Selection: Discuss methods for sizing and selecting components in hybrid powertrains, including electric motors, batteries, internal combustion engines, and power electronics, to meet performance and efficiency requirements.
    • Performance Evaluation and Testing: Explore techniques for evaluating the performance of hybrid electric vehicles through simulation, laboratory testing, and field trials, including performance metrics, data analysis, and validation against design specifications.
    • Hybrid Vehicle Safety and Reliability: Consider safety and reliability aspects of hybrid electric vehicles, including fault detection and diagnosis, fail-safe design, and risk assessment for hybrid powertrain components and systems.
    • Vehicle Integration and Packaging: Learn about the integration and packaging of hybrid powertrain components and systems into vehicle platforms, including layout optimization, thermal management, and space utilization considerations.
    • Environmental Impact Assessment: Conduct environmental impact assessments of hybrid electric vehicles, including life cycle analysis, emissions modeling, and environmental footprint estimation for vehicle production, operation, and disposal.
    • Regulatory Compliance and Certification: Understand regulatory requirements and certification processes for hybrid electric vehicles, including emissions standards, safety regulations, and compliance testing procedures.
    • Case Studies and Industry Applications: Explore real-world case studies and industry applications of hybrid electric vehicle design and analysis, including commercial HEV platforms, research prototypes, and innovative hybrid vehicle concepts.
    • Project Definition and Scope: Define the objectives, scope, and deliverables of the hybrid electric vehicle project, identifying specific design requirements, performance targets, and evaluation criteria.
    • Conceptual Design and Simulation: Develop conceptual designs for the hybrid electric vehicle prototype, including vehicle architecture, powertrain layout, component selection, and energy management strategies.
    • Component Integration and Packaging: Integrate and package hybrid powertrain components into the vehicle platform, optimizing layout, weight distribution, and packaging constraints for performance and safety.
    • Prototype Development and Testing: Build a physical prototype of the hybrid electric vehicle, including assembling components, wiring systems, and conducting functional tests to validate design integrity and performance.
    • Performance Evaluation and Optimization: Evaluate the performance of the hybrid electric vehicle prototype through laboratory testing, simulation analysis, and field trials, identifying areas for optimization and improvement.
    • Data Analysis and Interpretation: Analyze data collected from performance evaluations and tests, interpreting results, identifying trends, and drawing conclusions to assess design effectiveness and compliance with project objectives.
    • Documentation and Reporting: Document the hybrid electric vehicle project process, methodology, results, and findings in a comprehensive technical report, including design specifications, test procedures, and performance analysis.
    • Presentation and Communication: Present the hybrid electric vehicle project findings to stakeholders, peers, and industry professionals through oral presentations, demonstrations, and technical reports, effectively communicating design rationale, analysis results, and recommendations.
    • Reflection and Lessons Learned: Reflect on the hybrid electric vehicle project experience, identifying lessons learned, challenges encountered, and opportunities for further development and improvement in hybrid vehicle design and analysis.
    • Future Directions and Innovation: Explore future directions and innovations in hybrid electric vehicle technology, considering emerging trends, advancements in energy storage and propulsion systems, and potential applications in sustainable transportation.
