    Overview

    Satellite Communication Systems course explores the communication systems used in satellites and spacecraft for data transmission. Participants will learn about satellite orbits, link budget analysis, modulation techniques, and ground station operations. Through case studies and simulations, students will develop a comprehensive understanding of satellite communication protocols and technologies.

    Technologies Covered

    Satellite Communication Systems

    • Basics of satellite communication systems and their role in global connectivity.
    • Historical development and evolution of satellite technology.
    • Applications of satellite communication in broadcasting, telecommunication, navigation, and remote sensing.
    • Keplerian and Newtonian orbital mechanics.
    • Types of satellite orbits: geostationary, medium Earth orbit (MEO), low Earth orbit (LEO), and polar orbit.
    • Satellite launch vehicles and mission planning considerations.
    • Link budget calculation for satellite communication systems.
    • Path loss, atmospheric attenuation, and rain fade effects on satellite links.
    • Antenna gain and radiation pattern analysis for satellite ground stations and terminals.
    • Digital modulation schemes: amplitude shift keying (ASK), frequency shift keying (FSK), phase shift keying (PSK), and quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM).
    • Error detection and correction codes: forward error correction (FEC), convolutional codes, and Reed-Solomon codes.
    • Adaptive modulation and coding (AMC) for dynamic link adaptation.
