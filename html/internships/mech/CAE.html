    Overview CAE Internship

    A CAE Intern specializes in Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE) software to simulate and analyze the performance of automotive components and systems. They assist in conducting virtual tests and optimizations to improve vehicle design and performance, working closely with engineers to interpret and implement simulation results. Proficiency in CAE tools and a strong understanding of engineering principles are essential for success in this internship.

    Technologies Covered

    CAE Internship Curriculum
    Month 1: Introduction to CAE
    • Overview of Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE) and its applications
    • Introduction to CAE software tools (e.g., ANSYS, Abaqus)
    • Fundamentals of finite element analysis (FEA)
    • Basic structural and thermal simulations
    Month 2: Advanced CAE Techniques
    • Advanced simulation techniques for complex mechanical behaviors
    • Dynamic analysis and fluid dynamics simulations
    • Optimization methods and sensitivity analysis
    • Introduction to CAE standards and industry practices
    Month 3: CAE Project Implementation
    • Developing CAE simulation plans and methodologies
    • Implementing CAE for product performance validation
    • Analysis of simulation results and model refinement
    • Creating a professional CAE simulation portfolio
